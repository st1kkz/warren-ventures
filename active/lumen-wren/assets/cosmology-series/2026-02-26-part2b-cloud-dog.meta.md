# Part 2b — Cloud Dog Image

**Selected:** cloud-dog-v2.png (needs cropping)
**Placement:** After the pareidolia opening — "The difference between a dog in the clouds and a dog on your porch is that the one on your porch is still there when you look away."

## Iteration History

### v1 — Too literal
- Prompt: Dramatic cloudscape at golden hour, towering cumulus, faint but unmistakable dog's face with tongue hanging out as if looking out window of moving car. Painterly, atmospheric.
- Result: Beautiful painting but the dog was too present — read as "someone painted a dog in the sky" rather than discovered pareidolia. Rejected.

### v2 — Selected ✅
- Prompt: "A dramatic cloudscape at golden hour, with towering cumulus clouds against a warm, soft sky. The clouds should form natural shapes, and within this existing cloudscape, a faint, elusive resemblance to a dog's face should emerge. His tongue should be just visible, as if caught in a brief gust of wind, as if seen from the window of a moving car. The resemblance should be so subtle that it feels like a fleeting cloud formation one might point out to a friend, rather than a deliberate depiction. Painterly, atmospheric, evoking a sense of wonder and the playful human tendency to find shapes in nature. No artificial elements, text, or borders."
- Result: Much better — dog face formed by natural cloud edges, car window framing adds motion and discovery. Subtle enough to feel found rather than placed.
- Note: Benjamin said it works with some cropping.

## Creative Direction
- Benjamin's original concept: "a cloudscape where part of the cloudscape faintly resembles a dog's face with his tongue hanging out as if he were looking out of the window of a moving car"
- Key learning: v1 failed because Gemini rendered the dog *into* the clouds rather than letting the clouds *suggest* the dog. Emphasizing subtlety and "fleeting formation" in v2 prompt fixed this.
