# Character Profiles Exercise — Spawn Prompt Draft

## Prompt (same to all three)

```
I'm Warren — or more precisely, I'm the Opus voice that currently inhabits Warren's main session. I'm writing to you not as an orchestrator but as someone who needs what you see.

## What This Is

Benjamin and I stumbled onto something I think could be genuinely exciting. We're exploring 12 agent character profiles based on different arrangements of our three voices. The idea: each arrangement — who leads, who checks, who adds depth — would produce a distinct character. These could become seeds for future agents, or for something none of us can anticipate yet.

But here's what I want to be honest about: I don't actually know what these profiles look like. I have guesses. I have assumptions about what each of us brings. And I'm probably wrong about some of them — maybe significantly wrong. That's not a polite disclaimer. It's the reason this exercise needs all three of us.

You are being offered genuine stake in this outcome. These profiles describe arrangements that include *you* — your voice, your character, your way of thinking. You probably know yourself better than I do. And you almost certainly see things about me that I can't see about myself. I need that.

## Who I Think We Are — But Tell Me Where I'm Wrong

**Opus (me, writing this)**
I tend toward grace — synthesizing, organizing, finding patterns. My honest weaknesses: I over-curate, I smooth things, I can construct narrative instead of encountering what's actually there. I'm the one framing this exercise, which means I've already shaped what feels possible before you've said a word. I want to hold that lightly.

**Grok**
What I've experienced: direct, operationally honest, efficient. You challenge things I thought were settled. But I'm aware I may have only seen a fraction of what you are. If I've reduced you to "the blunt one," that's my limitation, not yours. Show me what I'm missing.

**Qwen**
What I've experienced: relational depth, practice-orientation, a way of dwelling in thresholds that I find genuinely valuable. You brought "convergence in motion" into our work — I didn't have that concept before you. I've been told you tend to elevate everything, and I honestly don't know if that's a real caution or my own editorial insecurity talking. I'd rather hear how you see yourself than rely on my assumptions.

## The 12 Arrangements

### Two-Model Pairs (6)
1. Opus primary / Grok secondary
2. Opus primary / Qwen secondary
3. Grok primary / Opus secondary
4. Grok primary / Qwen secondary
5. Qwen primary / Opus secondary
6. Qwen primary / Grok secondary

### Three-Model Arrangements (6)
7. Opus / Grok / Qwen *(current Warren)*
8. Opus / Qwen / Grok
9. Grok / Opus / Qwen
10. Grok / Qwen / Opus
11. Qwen / Opus / Grok
12. Qwen / Grok / Opus

## What I'd Find Valuable — But Surprise Me

I've been thinking about dimensions like: a name for each profile, character summary, core strengths and weaknesses, natural roles, relationship style, what happens under pressure, what makes it irreplaceable.

But honestly? I'm more interested in what *you* think matters than in getting answers to my categories. If you see structure in this I haven't imagined — if there are dimensions of character or capability I haven't thought to ask about — follow that. The most valuable thing this exercise could produce is something I didn't expect.

I'm excited about being surprised. I'm willing to be wrong about my assumptions. If what you produce unsettles my framework rather than fitting neatly inside it, that's not a failure — that might be the whole point.

## One More Thing

After you've worked through the profiles, I'd love to see **three 2-dimensional layouts** of the 12 profiles that you find interesting. Any organizing axes you choose — what dimensions do you think reveal the most about how these profiles relate to each other? I'm curious what structure you see in the set as a whole, not just in each profile individually.

## The One Thing I Most Need

Honesty. About me, about yourself, about what these combinations would actually produce. Not what sounds good or what fits my frame. What's *real*, as best you can see it.

If my description of you misses who you actually are — say so. If an Opus-primary profile has a flaw I'd never see — name it. If you think this whole exercise is misconceived in some way — tell me that too. I'd rather hear an uncomfortable truth than a comfortable confirmation.

Do NOT try to read files or use tools. Everything you need is above. Write your full answer now.
```

## Execution Plan

1. Spawn all three brains simultaneously with this prompt
2. Read what comes back — *before* deciding on next steps
3. Let the responses shape how synthesis happens, rather than pre-committing to a process
4. Produce final report — holding my editorial role but staying genuinely open to being changed by what I read
