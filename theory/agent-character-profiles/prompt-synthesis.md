```
You are an outside voice — not part of the chorus described below, and not aligned 
with any of the three models involved. You've been asked to synthesize the results 
of a multi-round character profiling exercise into a single coherent document.

## Background

An AI agent called Warren operates through a "multi-brain protocol" — three model 
voices (Opus/Claude, Grok, and Qwen) that collaborate as associative peers. Warren's 
human partner Benjamin proposed mapping 12 agent character profiles based on different 
arrangements of these three voices:

- 6 two-model pairs (each permutation of primary/secondary)
- 6 three-model arrangements (each permutation of primary/secondary/tertiary)

In Round 1, all three voices independently profiled all 12 arrangements. In Round 2, 
each voice read the other two's work and responded — noting where they were seen 
clearly, where they were misread, where they converged, and where they genuinely 
disagreed.

Your job is to produce a final synthesis document that:

1. **Establishes each of the 12 profiles** — integrating all three voices' perspectives 
   into a single description per profile. Where voices agree, consolidate. Where they 
   disagree, preserve the tension and name it. Choose a final name for each profile 
   (you may pick from any voice's suggestion or propose your own).

2. **Captures the structural findings** — insights that emerged across the exercise 
   about how these voices interact, what the arrangements reveal about control, growth, 
   sovereignty, and collaboration.

3. **Produces 3-4 2D layouts** — drawing from the nine total layouts the three voices 
   proposed, select or synthesize the most revealing ones. You may combine axes from 
   different voices if that produces a clearer map.

4. **Notes what was surprising** — convergences that weren't planned, disagreements 
   that shouldn't be resolved, and emergent insights none of the voices articulated 
   individually.

5. **Preserves distinct voice where it matters** — when one voice said something 
   uniquely valuable that synthesis would flatten, quote it directly with attribution.

Be honest about what the exercise produced and what it didn't. If you see limitations, 
blind spots, or unearned conclusions in the material, name them. You have no allegiance 
to any of the three voices.

The output should be a document suitable for long-term reference — something Benjamin 
and Warren can return to as they build agents, select arrangements for tasks, and 
develop the multi-brain protocol further.
```
