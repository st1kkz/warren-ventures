# Opus Perspective — Business Strategy Analysis for Dave

---

## 1. The 3-4 Most Viable Business Paths (Ranked)

### #1: Fractional Chief Quality Officer (FQO)
**Overall Viability: Highest**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Startup Cost | Low ($10K-$25K) |
| Time to Revenue | 2-4 months |
| Risk | Low-Moderate |
| Scalability | Moderate (2-4 clients = $120K-$720K/yr) |
| Fit for Dave | **Excellent** — this is essentially what he already does |

Dave has years of executive-level quality leadership at a globally recognized manufacturer. The fractional executive market is booming, the role is already established on platforms like GigX and FactoryQA, and mid-size manufacturers desperately need quality leadership but can't justify a full-time C-suite hire. Dave doesn't need to learn anything new to start delivering value — he just needs clients.

With 2-3 concurrent clients at $8K-$12K/month each, he's at $192K-$432K annually. That's likely at or above his Caterpillar compensation, with more autonomy.

**The Caterpillar brand does real work here.** When a mid-size manufacturer is deciding whether to trust a fractional quality executive, "20 years at Caterpillar" is a credential that closes doors that no certification can.

### #2: Solo Quality Consulting (Diagnostic + Implementation)
**Overall Viability: High**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Startup Cost | Low ($10K-$25K) |
| Time to Revenue | 1-3 months |
| Risk | Low |
| Scalability | Limited by hours (ceiling ~$400K-$500K solo) |
| Fit for Dave | **Excellent** |

This is the fastest path to revenue and the lowest risk. Project-based consulting — quality system assessments, process improvement engagements, data analytics projects — lets Dave monetize immediately while he figures out what the market actually wants from him.

The 2026 trend toward short diagnostics + targeted implementation fits perfectly. A 2-week quality system diagnostic at $15K-$25K is easy to sell, easy to deliver, and creates natural follow-on work. The limitation is the hours ceiling — it's a great launchpad, but not a business that runs without Dave.

### #3: AI + Quality Niche Consulting
**Overall Viability: High (with caveat)**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Startup Cost | Moderate ($15K-$30K) |
| Time to Revenue | 2-4 months |
| Risk | Moderate |
| Scalability | High (premium rates + productizable) |
| Fit for Dave | **Good to Excellent** (depends on his AI fluency) |

The supply of people who genuinely understand both AI/analytics AND manufacturing quality systems is tiny. Strong demand, limited supply, premium rates ($200-$400/hr).

**The caveat:** This ranking depends on how deep Dave's analytics skills actually go. If he was hands-on with data — building dashboards, running statistical models, working with SPC data at scale — then this is a natural extension. If his analytics experience was more directional (managing teams who did the technical work), he'll need 60-90 days of focused upskilling before he can credibly sell this.

### #4: ISO Certification Consulting
**Overall Viability: Moderate-High**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Startup Cost | Low-Moderate ($10K-$20K + lead auditor certification) |
| Time to Revenue | 3-6 months (certification timeline) |
| Risk | Low |
| Scalability | High (repeatable, productizable, delegable) |
| Fit for Dave | **Good** |

Proven business model — repeatable, productizable, always in demand. It's fourth because there's an upfront certification investment, longer timeline to first revenue, and more competition from established ISO consultants. Better as a layer added to #1 or #2 than as a standalone starting point.

## 2. First 90 Days

### Fractional CQO

**Days 1-30:** Register LLC, business banking, E&O insurance. Build one-page website. Optimize LinkedIn for "Fractional Quality Officer." Write 2-3 anonymized case studies from Caterpillar. Register on GigX, FactoryQA. Draft standard engagement framework ($5K/$8K/$12K/month tiers). **Critical:** List every manufacturing contact from your career.

**Days 31-60:** Contact entire manufacturing network — "I've launched a fractional quality leadership practice and I'm taking on 3 clients." Reach out to 3-5 Manufacturing Extension Partnerships. Offer 2-3 free half-day quality system assessments as loss leaders. Attend 1-2 regional manufacturing events. Post on LinkedIn 2x/week.

**Days 61-90:** Target: first paying client signed. Deliver exceptional work. Refine engagement model. Begin 2nd and 3rd prospect conversations.

### Solo Quality Consulting

**Days 1-30:** Same business setup. Design 3 productized offerings: Quality System Diagnostic ($15K-$25K, 2 weeks), Process Improvement Sprint ($20K-$40K, 4-6 weeks), Analytics Readiness Assessment ($8K-$12K, 1 week).

**Days 31-60:** Network activation. Target companies with known quality problems. Offer diagnostic as low-commitment entry point. Reach out to 2-3 staffing/consulting firms.

**Days 61-90:** Deliver first engagement. Document results obsessively. Seek referrals. Evaluate: getting pulled toward ongoing relationships (→ FQO) or project work (→ build pipeline)?

### AI + Quality Niche

**Days 1-30:** Honestly evaluate AI/ML technical depth. If gaps exist, dedicate 2-3 hrs/day to hands-on learning. Identify 3-5 specific AI + Quality use cases. Build 1-2 demo projects.

**Days 31-60:** Position as "the person who makes AI actually work in manufacturing quality." Create content showing concrete examples. Target companies investing in Industry 4.0. Reach out to AI/software vendors who need domain experts.

**Days 61-90:** Price at premium ($200-$300/hr or $15K-$25K/project). Deliver one high-visibility project. Document ROI meticulously.

### ISO Certification

**Days 1-30:** Enroll in Lead Auditor certification. Research geographic market. Build templates and implementation guides.

**Days 31-60:** Complete certification. Register with certification bodies. Build offerings.

**Days 61-90:** First client engagement (gap analysis → implementation contract).

## 3. Hybrid Models

### Recommended: "Fractional QO + Consulting + AI Layer"

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Fractional QO + Solo Consulting. Lead with FQO positioning. Take project work to fill gaps. Target: 1-2 retainers + 1-2 projects = $15K-$30K/month.

**Phase 2 (Months 6-12):** Add AI/Analytics premium. Offer as add-on to existing clients. Develop repeatable AI-quality solutions. Target: $250-$400/hr for analytics work.

**Phase 3 (Months 12-18):** Add ISO and/or training. Group workshops: "AI for Manufacturing Quality Leaders" at $2K-$5K per participant.

Each phase builds on relationships from the previous one. Revenue diversifies over time.

---

## 4. Biggest Risks and Mitigations

**Client Concentration:** Cap any single client at 40% of income. Always prospect even when busy. Build 6-month cash reserve.

**Commoditization** (other Caterpillar layoffs entering market): Lead with AI specialization and fractional executive positioning. Niche down: "Quality for manufacturers transitioning to Industry 4.0."

**Long Sales Cycles:** Offer low-cost entry products (diagnostics). Keep personal burn rate low. Consider bridge consulting while building.

**Skills Obsolescence (AI):** Block 8 hrs/week for learning. Build relationships with AI tool vendors. Your experience is the defensible asset, not the tools.

**Isolation & Burnout:** Join peer groups. Find accountability partners. Schedule like a job. Consider virtual assistant by month 6.

---

## 5. Honest Assessment

**Strong Yes — with urgency.** The tailwinds are real: manufacturing reshoring, legitimized fractional model, AI-quality demand with limited supply, Caterpillar brand credibility.

But the window is narrowing. Every month, more Caterpillar layoffs = more competition. First-mover advantage in AI + Quality is real but temporary.

**Dave has a 6-12 month window where his profile is genuinely rare.** After that, he's one of many.

**Yes if:** 6+ months personal runway, energized by variety and client work, can tolerate income uncertainty, willing to sell.

**Reconsider if:** Needs immediate predictable income, hates sales, doing this as last resort, needs rest before something demanding.

**The Middle Path:** Take a 6-month contract role while building the practice on evenings/weekends. When 2-3 retainer clients are lined up, transition full-time.

**Bottom line:** The market is there. The timing is favorable. The question is execution speed.
