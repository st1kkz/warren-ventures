## Tier 3 — The Reshoring Wave (New Domestic Suppliers)

**This is the real gold rush.** Cat is actively bringing sourcing back to the US due to tariff pressure. New domestic suppliers stepping into the Cat supply chain need to meet SQEP requirements from scratch:

- ISO 9001 / IATF 16949 quality management systems
- APQP, FMEA, PPAP, SPC competency
- CPk requirements for special characteristics
- 100% on-time delivery tracking
- PPM quality levels approaching single digits for higher SQEP tiers

**These companies don't know what they don't know.** They've been making parts for smaller customers and suddenly Cat's SQE team shows up with a 200-page audit checklist.

**How to find them:** Watch for companies in Cat hub cities (see Tier 4) that are adding Cat to their customer base. Trade shows (IMTS, FABTECH), local manufacturing associations, and SBA/MEP centers will know who's newly onboarding.

## The SQEP Pain Point (Dave's Wedge)

Caterpillar's Supplier Quality Excellence Process is *brutal* for small manufacturers:

- **Bronze** requires: ISO-level systems, APQP/FMEA/PPAP/SPC competency, delivery metrics, CPk tracking
- **Silver and Gold**: progressively harder — PPM < 10, on-time delivery = 100%, CPk > 2.0
- **Platinum**: "as close to perfection as possible"
- Certification reviewed annually by a cross-functional global Cat team (engineering, manufacturing, purchasing, quality)
- **Losing certification means losing volume.** Cat shifts orders to certified suppliers.

A small shop with 50 employees and a quality manager who's really a production supervisor with extra duties? They're drowning. They don't need a consultant with templates. They need someone who has *sat in those Cat audit rooms* and knows exactly what the auditors want to see.
